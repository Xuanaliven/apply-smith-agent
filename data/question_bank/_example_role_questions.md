# Question Bank: Operations Role (Generic)

> This is a sample question bank for a generic operations role.
> Create role-specific and company-specific versions as you build your pipeline.
> Mark questions you have prepared answers for with [x].

---

## Background & Motivation

- [ ] Walk me through your background and why you are interested in this role.
- [ ] Why operations specifically, rather than product or data roles?
- [ ] What does a typical day look like in your current/most recent role?
- [ ] What has been your biggest career achievement so far?
- [ ] Where do you see yourself in 3 years, and how does this role fit that path?

---

## Role Understanding

- [ ] What do you think the core responsibility of this role is?
- [ ] How do you think operations contributes to the company's overall business performance?
- [ ] What do you think is the most important metric for measuring success in this role?
- [ ] What do you think differentiates a good operations person from a great one?
- [ ] What would you prioritize in your first 90 days if you joined this team?

---

## Behavioral / STAR Format

> For each question, prepare a 2-minute answer using the STAR structure:
> **Situation** → **Task** → **Action** → **Result**

- [ ] Tell me about a time you had to manage competing priorities under a tight deadline.
- [ ] Describe a situation where you identified a process inefficiency and took action to fix it.
- [ ] Tell me about a time you had to work with a team that had different goals from yours.
- [ ] Describe a project where the initial plan did not work and you had to adapt.
- [ ] Tell me about a time you used data to change someone's mind or influence a decision.

---

## Case / Scenario

- [ ] Our DAU dropped 15% last Monday compared to the previous Monday. How would you investigate this?
- [ ] You have three initiatives to improve user retention, but resources for only one. How do you choose?
- [ ] A key campaign is launching in 3 days, but the data shows one creative underperforms. What do you do?
- [ ] You notice a metric is trending in the wrong direction for two weeks. No one else has flagged it. What are your next steps?
- [ ] Your team wants to launch a new user incentive program. How would you design it and how would you measure success?

---

## Data & Metrics

- [ ] What metrics would you use to measure the health of a user operations function?
- [ ] How would you distinguish between a seasonal dip and a structural problem in retention data?
- [ ] If you had to pick one metric to represent the overall performance of this team, what would it be and why?
- [ ] How do you decide whether a change in a metric is meaningful or just noise?
- [ ] Walk me through how you would design a reporting structure for a new initiative.

---

## Questions to Ask Your Interviewer

> Asking thoughtful questions signals business understanding and genuine interest.
> Avoid questions that can be answered by reading the careers page.

- [ ] What does success look like for this role in the first 6 months?
- [ ] What is the biggest challenge the team is currently navigating?
- [ ] How does this team collaborate with the product / data / marketing team day-to-day?
- [ ] What does the career path typically look like for someone who joins at this level?
- [ ] How does the company decide which new operations initiatives to prioritize?
