# Industry Map: Finance / Securities / Fund / Banking / Fintech

## Overview

The finance sector encompasses securities brokerage, public and private fund management, commercial banking, investment banking, insurance, and fintech platforms. Sub-sectors differ sharply in culture, compensation, work style, and candidate profiles. Understand which sub-sector you are targeting before building materials.

---

## Sub-Sector Overview

| Sub-Sector | Business Logic | Culture | Candidate Profile |
|------------|---------------|---------|------------------|
| Securities / Brokerage | Commission, proprietary trading, IB fees | Project-driven, deadline-heavy, client-facing | CFA/CPA interest, financial modeling, deal stamina |
| Public Fund (公募) | Management fee on AUM | Research-heavy, long-horizon thinking | Strong research skills, sector depth, writing ability |
| Private Equity / VC | Carried interest on returns | Relationship-driven, deal-sourcing | Top-tier background, network, financial modeling |
| Commercial Bank | Net interest margin, fee income | Compliance-heavy, process-oriented | Risk awareness, regulatory literacy, patience |
| Fintech Platform | Transaction fee, data monetization | Tech-forward, product-driven | Tech + finance hybrid, user-centric thinking |
| Insurance | Premium income, investment return | Long sales cycles, compliance | Communication, actuarial interest (for technical roles) |

---

## Typical Companies

- Mid-size securities brokerage (investment banking + brokerage + research)
- Leading public fund manager (equity, fixed income, index funds)
- Large state-owned bank (retail banking + corporate banking + wealth management)
- Fintech payment platform (third-party payment, consumer credit, wealth management app)
- Foreign investment bank (institutional sales, structuring, compliance-heavy)
- Hedge fund / quantitative fund (systematic strategies, tech-heavy)

## Required Capabilities

- Financial modeling: DCF, comparable company analysis, LBO basics (IB-track)
- Regulatory knowledge: relevant laws, compliance frameworks, CSRC/PBOC/CBIRC oversight
- Data analysis: Excel/Python, portfolio analytics, performance attribution
- Research writing: sector analysis, investment thesis, company deep-dives
- Risk reasoning: credit risk, market risk, operational risk frameworks

## Interview Focus

- **Case studies**: value a company, analyze a sector thesis, size a market
- **Market knowledge**: current macro environment, sector trends, policy changes
- **Ethical reasoning**: how would you handle a conflict of interest? What is your compliance mindset?
- **Technical modeling**: walk me through a DCF or a valuation approach
- **Behavioral**: describe a time you navigated ambiguity or worked under deadline pressure

---

## Suitable Candidate Profile

- Finance, economics, or mathematics majors with strong analytical fundamentals
- Candidates with CFA Level 1+ for research and investment tracks
- CS or data science backgrounds for fintech and quant roles
- Candidates who can write clearly and think rigorously about uncertainty

---

## Risk Notes

- **Cycle sensitivity**: finance hiring is tightly correlated with market conditions; headcount can freeze without warning
- **Role ceiling clarity**: some operational roles in banking have limited upward mobility without a specialized credential track
- **Regulatory exposure**: any compliance failure affects the entire institution and can result in sudden role elimination
- **Culture variation**: state-owned bank culture differs sharply from a foreign IB or fintech startup — assess fit before applying
- **Hours**: IB and research roles have reputationally high hours, especially during deal season or reporting periods
