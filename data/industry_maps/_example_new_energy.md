# Industry Map: New Energy / EV / Photovoltaic

## Overview

The new energy sector covers electric vehicles, battery technology, photovoltaic (solar) manufacturing, wind power, and energy storage systems. It is one of the fastest-growing industrial sectors, heavily shaped by government policy, supply chain dynamics, and global decarbonization commitments.

---

## Typical Companies

- Leading EV manufacturer (full-stack: design, manufacturing, software, sales)
- Major battery manufacturer (power battery and energy storage battery)
- Large photovoltaic module producer (upstream silicon wafer to downstream module)
- Energy storage system integrator (grid-side and user-side storage)
- Emerging EV startup (software-defined vehicle, direct-to-consumer sales)
- Inverter and power electronics manufacturer
- Charging infrastructure operator

> Note: Use company descriptions, not specific names, when discussing sensitive competitive positions.

## Business Model

- **Product sales**: EV units, battery packs, solar modules
- **Energy service**: BESS (battery energy storage system) leasing, virtual power plant
- **Software / ecosystem**: OTA subscriptions, in-car services, charging network memberships
- **Carbon credits / trading**: carbon offset monetization in regulated markets

---

## Required Capabilities

- Supply chain thinking: BOM cost, capacity planning, supplier qualification
- Cost analysis: LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy), per-kWh economics, yield rate optimization
- Policy literacy: carbon neutrality roadmaps, government subsidy structures, EV purchase incentives
- Cross-functional collaboration: hardware teams, software teams, regulatory teams
- Data analysis: production metrics, quality control data, sales forecasting

## Risk Notes

- **Policy sensitivity**: sector is heavily dependent on government subsidies and regulations; rapid policy change can reshape the landscape
- **Rapid headcount swings**: companies scale fast during boom cycles and cut quickly during downturns
- **Role drift**: operational roles can become extremely tactical if the team is resource-constrained
- **Intense internal competition**: high-talent-density environments in top EV firms mean promotion is slow
- **Geographic concentration**: many roles are in manufacturing hubs (Shenzhen, Shanghai, Hefei, Changsha) â€” consider relocation willingness
