# Role Schema: Advertising Operations

## Role Overview

Advertising Operations (Ad Ops) manages the setup, execution, monitoring, and optimization of paid advertising campaigns. The role sits at the intersection of data, platform operations, and business growth. Common in internet platforms, e-commerce companies, agencies, and brands with in-house media buying teams.

---

## Sub-Roles

| Sub-Role | Focus | Common Employer Type |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| Campaign Manager | Day-to-day campaign execution and pacing | Brand, agency, platform |
| Performance Analyst | Data-driven bid and budget optimization | Platform, e-commerce |
| Ad Platform Ops | Advertiser support, policy, quality | Ad platform |
| Programmatic Ops | DSP/SSP management, audience targeting | Agency, large brand |
| Search Ops | Search keyword bidding and copy management | E-commerce, agency |

---

## Responsibilities

- Set up and launch advertising campaigns across search, social, display, and video channels
- Monitor daily budget pacing, impression delivery, and campaign health
- Analyze performance metrics and adjust bids, targeting, and creatives to meet KPIs
- Run A/B tests on ad copy, landing pages, audience segments, and bid strategies
- Build and maintain performance dashboards and recurring reports
- Troubleshoot delivery issues, disapproved creatives, and tracking failures
- Coordinate with creative, product, and finance teams on campaign needs
- Compile weekly/monthly performance reports with recommendations

---

## Core Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| CTR | Click-through rate (clicks / impressions) | Ad relevance and quality |
| CVR | Conversion rate (conversions / clicks) | Landing page and audience fit |
| ROAS | Return on ad spend (revenue / spend) | Efficiency of paid media |
| ACOS | Advertising cost of sales (spend / revenue) | E-commerce ad efficiency |
| CPC | Cost per click | Bid competitiveness |
| CPM | Cost per thousand impressions | Awareness campaign efficiency |
| CPA | Cost per acquisition | Performance campaign efficiency |
| Impression Share | Your impressions / total eligible impressions | Competitive coverage |
| Budget Utilization | Actual spend / planned budget | Pacing health |
| Quality Score | Platform scoring (ad relevance + landing page) | Influences CPC |

## Required Tools

- **Ad platforms**: Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, ByteDance / TikTok Ads, Baidu Promotion, Kuaishou Ads, programmatic DSPs
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics, BI dashboards (Tableau, Looker, or internal tools)
- **Productivity**: Excel/Sheets (pivot tables, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP), PowerPoint
- **Basic SQL**: querying campaign logs, building custom attribution reports
- **Tracking**: UTM parameters, pixel implementation basics, attribution model concepts

---

## Interview Focus

- **Metrics interpretation**: "CTR is high but CVR is low — what do you investigate?"
- **Case scenario**: "A campaign is 30% behind pacing with 2 days left. What do you do?"
- **Platform knowledge**: Walk me through how you would set up a lead-gen campaign
- **A/B testing**: "How would you test two creative variants fairly?"
- **Reporting**: "What metrics would you include in a weekly campaign summary for a client?"

---

## Portfolio Suggestions

1. **Mock Campaign Analysis**: Take a public dataset (e.g., Kaggle ad spend datasets) and produce a campaign performance report with findings and recommendations
2. **Optimization Exercise**: Show a before/after scenario where you improved a metric — document your hypothesis, change made, and result
3. **Dashboard Build**: Create a performance dashboard in Google Sheets or a free BI tool using sample data
4. **A/B Test Write-up**: Design a test (even hypothetically), state your hypothesis, describe how you would measure significance, and interpret mock results
